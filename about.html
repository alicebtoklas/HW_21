About Me

My first user experience crash course was the university classroom. Interacting with students with different backgrounds, interests, and learning needs taught me to listen empathetically, ask the right questions, and iterate my teaching materials based on student feedback.

I am also a writer, researcher, and PhD. I’ve written about contemporary queer fiction, and I’ve interviewed writers about their work. In my free time I volunteer in community projects, most recently working as a volunteer interviewer for an oral history project with the Lesbian Herstory Archives.

I bring my passion for understanding people’s motivations, expectations, and desires to my work as a UX researcher. As a qualitative researcher, I aim to breathe life into data and to understand the day-to-day human impact behind our design decisions.
